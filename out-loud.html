Lived experience

Writing that started as therapy and felt too important not to share.

My name is Nic Whippey. I am a neurodivergent parent of a neurodivergent child, and I write about what that actually looks like — not the polished version, but the real one.

These pieces started as my way of processing what was happening to my family: the school that couldn’t see him, the system that wouldn’t help, the burnout that nearly broke me. I wrote to make sense of it. Then I realised other families were going through the same thing, in silence, and I decided to share.

I write about neurodivergent parenting, masking, meltdowns, the SEND system, late diagnosis, neuroscience, hypermobility, and the impossible maths of holding it all together when every system around you is failing. I use my background in neuroscience to try to understand the biology behind what we experience, and my background in software engineering to try to fix the systems that don’t work.
