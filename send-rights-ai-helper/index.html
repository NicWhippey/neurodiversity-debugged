What this tool does

💬

Listens to your story

The AI asks open questions and lets you talk. It does not rush you. It does not judge. It pieces together the full picture — your child, the timeline, what has failed, and what it has cost you.

⚖

Explains your rights in plain English

Most parents do not know the law is on their side. The tool explains what duties the council and school have, what you can do right now, and where to get free legal advice.

✍

Writes a formal complaint letter

If you want one. Led by your real experience, in your voice, backed by law in footnotes. The kind of letter that gets a response.

📌

Connects you with help

IPSEA, SENDIAS, your local MP, Not Fine in School, and more — specific to your area.

What this tool does not do

This is not legal advice. It is AI-generated information based on publicly available legal sources. It may contain errors. Use it at your own risk and verify key points with IPSEA or a solicitor before taking formal action.

It does not diagnose your child. It is not a substitute for professional legal, medical, or psychological support. It covers England only — Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have different legal frameworks.

Why AI?

There is not a single professional who has the time to understand the complexities and lengths you have taken to help your child. Not one person who can sit with you long enough to uncover all the things you already know — buried under years of meetings, emails, assessments, and crisis — and help you put it all out clearly.

There is no one to debug what you have done. To go back through everything that has happened and find the flaws in the system you have encountered. No one to help you unpick it all, in your own time, so you can advocate better for yourself and your child.

This tool does that. It helps you summarise everything — your child’s needs, your timeline, your experiences — in a way that is impossible to do on your own, because none of us are lawyers, healthcare professionals, and education specialists all at once. And here is the truth: there is not a single professional who knows all of these things either. No one joins the dots between education, health, and social care — except you, the parent, holding it all together.

AI can. It can sit with you at 2am when IPSEA is closed and SENDIAS has a waiting list. It can explain things three different ways without losing patience. It can take everything you tell it and help you see the full picture — the legal rights, the duties that have been breached, the remedies you are entitled to — and help you put that into words that get heard.

AI is not perfect. It can make mistakes. It is not a lawyer. This tool says that clearly, and it tells you where to go for proper legal advice. But for a parent who does not even know they have rights, an AI that can say “the council has a legal duty to educate your child, and here is how you hold them to it” is transformative.

This is not about replacing human support. It is about helping you join the dots to get what you and your family are legally entitled to.
